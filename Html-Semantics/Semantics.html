Googling Through Life

I asked you once, “Can cats eat cheese?”
You answered fast with expert ease.
Then down the rabbit hole I fell,
Now I know how otters smell.

I searched for “how to fix my sink,”
You gave me ads before a link.
Then somehow, two clicks down the line,
I’m learning facts on porcupine.

You heard me sneeze? You showed me flu.
You spy? You swear it isn’t true…
Yet every time I misspell stuff,
You finish—creepily enough.

Oh Google, friend that’s always near,
Just stop with “ads for pants,” my dear.
I only asked for pie recipes—
Why am I shopping Japanese tees?

Fun facts about G o o g l e
  1. Google’s original logo colors (blue, red, yellow, green) can be loosely associated with the elements in a flame test,
    which is a chemical method used to identify metal ions based on the color they emit when burned:
    • Blue - Could be linked to copper (Cu2+), which gives a blue-green flame.
    • Red - Strontium (Sr2+) or lithium (Li+) produces a red flame.
    • Yellow - Sodium (Na+) emits a strong yellow flame.
    • Green - Boron (in compounds) or barium (Ba2+) gives a green flame.

    So, in a way, Google's colorful branding could be seen as a vibrant chemical flame test — not intentional, but a cool crossover between tech and science!

  2. The name "Google" is derived from the mathematical term "googol", which is 10100 - 1 followed by 100 zeros.
    It was coined by a 9-year-old nephew of mathematician Edward Kasner.

  3. Google’s first storage was built from LEGO®.
    In 1996, Google founders Larry Page and Sergey Brin built their first server storage using LEGO bricks to house ten 4GB hard drives — totaling 40GB. Creative and nerdy!

  4. The phrase “To google” became a verb in 2006.
    It can be officially found in the Oxford English Dictionary.
    google (verb) = to search for information on the internet using the Google search engine

  5. Search "askew" or "tilt" on Google
    The page actually tilts slightly! A cheeky nod to geometry and angles.

  6. Search “Google in 1998”
    It takes you back to the retro version of Google’s homepage — great for tech nostalgia.

  7. There's a Google Easter Eggs website called elgoog
